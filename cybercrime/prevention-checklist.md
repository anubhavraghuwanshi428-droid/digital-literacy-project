# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

## General Safety Tips (6 tips)
1. **Verify before you click** – Always check the sender’s email address. Legitimate organizations use official domains (e.g., @company.com, @college.edu), not Gmail or Yahoo.
2. **Enable Two‑Factor Authentication (2FA)** – Use 2FA on all important accounts (email, social media, banking) to add an extra layer of security.
3. **Use strong, unique passwords** – Never reuse passwords across platforms. Use a password manager if needed.
4. **Think before sharing** – Do not post sensitive information like your phone number, address, or college ID publicly on social media.
5. **Be cautious with links** – Hover over links before clicking to see the actual URL. If it looks suspicious, do not click.
6. **Avoid public Wi‑Fi for sensitive transactions** – Do not access banking, UPI, or email accounts on unsecured public networks. Use mobile data or a VPN if necessary.

## Financial / UPI Safety Tips (2 tips)
7. **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP** – No legitimate authority (bank, college, company) will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP via email, phone, or message.
8. **Use UPI with caution** – Only scan QR codes from trusted sources. Avoid sending money to “verification” or “fee refund” requests from unknown contacts.
